# Ollantaytambo – Festival of the Señor de Choquekillka

This repository hosts photographs of **Ollantaytambo** during the **Festival of the Señor de Choquekillka**, the town’s most important religious and cultural celebration. The images capture the devotion, dances, and community spirit that make this feast a highlight of the Sacred Valley.

---

## 📖 About the Festival
- **Patron Saint**: The Señor de Choquekillka, a sacred cross venerated in Ollantaytambo.
- **Origins**: Tradition says the cross appeared miraculously in the Vilcanota River at Choqekillkayoq in the early 20th century.
- **Dates**: Celebrated annually beginning on the **first Sunday of Pentecost**.  
  - **2026 Date**: Sunday, **May 24th, 2026**.
- **Recognition**: Declared *Patrimonio Cultural de la Nación* (Cultural Heritage of the Nation) in 2008.

---

## 🎉 Main Events
- **Processions**: The cross is carried between its chapel and the Santiago Apóstol church, accompanied by music and prayers.
- **Dances**: Traditional comparsas (troupes) perform, including Qhapac Qolla, Qhapac Negro, Mestiza Qoyacha, and Panaderas.
- **Liturgies**: Masses, vigils, and the “Alba general” (dawn ceremony) form the spiritual core of the feast.
- **Community Participation**: Families and institutions decorate streets with floral carpets, organize fireworks, bull runs, and cultural exhibitions.

## 🌍 Cultural Significance
The festival blends **Catholic devotion** with **Andean traditions**, symbolizing Ollantaytambo’s living Inca heritage. It unites locals, pilgrims, and visitors in a shared celebration of identity, faith, and artistry.
